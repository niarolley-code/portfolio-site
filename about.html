About

Baltimore raised me. Spelman sharpened me.

I grew up in Baltimore — a city that doesn’t get the credit it deserves and a city I’ve spent my whole life showing up for.

I’m a Spelman College alumna, a member of Sigma Gamma Rho Sorority Inc., a first-place research award recipient, and a National Society of Leadership and Success Scholar. But those aren’t the things that define me. What defines me is the way I move through any room I enter — always asking what could be better, brighter, more efficient, more human.

“You are not the work you do; you are the person you are.”

— Toni Morrison

I’m currently pursuing a Master’s in City and Regional Planning at Morgan State University — and honestly, it feels less like a new direction and more like a name finally being put on something I’ve always done. I’ve spent years working at the intersection of housing equity, civic engagement, public health, education, and community development. The planning degree is where all of those threads formalize into a framework. It’s where my instincts meet their infrastructure.

My lens is specific and I don’t shy away from that. As a Black woman, I approach everything through intersectionality — which means nothing I produce is without nuance, and no community I work in is treated as a monolith. I think about who cities are built for and who gets left out of that conversation. I think about which neighborhoods absorb environmental harm while others absorb investment. I think about what it costs a child to grow up in a school system that wasn’t designed with them in mind.

I’ve worked in classrooms with six-year-olds and in boardrooms with executives. I’ve built campaigns, coordinated convenings, written policy frameworks, and led teams. I’m also a writer, a creative, and someone who believes deeply that how a story is told determines whether it changes anything.

Baltimore raised me. Spelman sharpened me. Morgan State is giving me the language for what I’ve always been building toward.
